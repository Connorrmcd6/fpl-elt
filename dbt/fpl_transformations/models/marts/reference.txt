dim_players

Purpose: A single, clean table for all player attributes.
Source: int_players_enriched
Columns: player_id, full_name, team_name, position, current_cost, status, etc.
Use Case: Filtering and grouping analysis by player, team, or position.
dim_teams

Purpose: A detailed reference for each team.
Source: stg_teams
Columns: team_id, team_name, team_short_name, strength metrics, etc.
Use Case: Analyzing team-level performance and fixture difficulty.
dim_gameweeks

Purpose: Describes each gameweek.
Source: stg_gameweeks
Columns: gameweek_id, gameweek_name, deadline_time, is_current, average_score, etc.
Use Case: Analyzing trends over time, week by week.
dim_fixtures

Purpose: A complete view of every match played.
Source: int_fixtures_with_teams
Columns: fixture_id, gameweek_id, kickoff_time, home_team_name, away_team_name, home_score, away_score, result.
Use Case: Analyzing match results and team matchups.
Fact Tables (The "Measurements")
These tables capture events and metrics. They are often large and contain foreign keys to your dimension tables.

fct_player_gameweek_performance

Purpose: The core of your FPL analysis. This table would track every player's performance in every gameweek they participated in.
Source: This would be a more complex transformation, likely parsing the stats_json from stg_fixtures and joining it with dim_players and dim_gameweeks.
Grain: One row per player per gameweek.
Columns: player_id (FK), fixture_id (FK), gameweek_id (FK), minutes_played, goals_scored, assists, clean_sheets, bonus_points, total_points.
Use Case: This is your main table for analyzing player form, points, and value over time.
fct_team_results

Purpose: To aggregate team-level results for each fixture.
Source: dim_fixtures
Grain: One row per team per fixture.
Columns: team_id (FK), fixture_id (FK), gameweek_id (FK), was_home_team, goals_scored, goals_conceded, points (3 for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss).
Use Case: Creating league tables, analyzing team form, and home/away performance.
